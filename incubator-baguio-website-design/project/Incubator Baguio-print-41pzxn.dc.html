About the Alliance

The missing link between
ideas and impact.

Incubator Baguio is the city's institutional home for innovation, connecting government, academe, industry, and the community under one alliance to turn local talent into lasting value.

Our Mission

To build a dynamic, inclusive, and authoritative platform that turns Baguio's intellectual capital into economic and social value.

Our Vision

A Baguio recognized as a leading hub for research, innovation, and homegrown startups in the Cordillera and beyond.

One alliance, four forces

Built on the quadruple helix

Government

The City Government convenes the alliance and anchors it in policy and public mandate.

Academe

Universities supply research, talent, and the labs where new ideas are tested.

Industry

Companies bring markets, mentorship, and capital to scale what works.

Community

Civil society and the public ground the work in real local needs.

City Hall Imagery
Our Mandate

A public institution, by ordinance

Incubator Baguio was operationalized under Ordinance No. 63, Series of 2023, establishing the Baguio City Research and Innovation Alliance under the City Planning, Development and Sustainability Office (CPDSO).

That mandate makes the work durable. It is not a one-off project but a standing commitment of the City Government to nurture innovation for the long term.

Innovation Camp

A high-energy annual immersion for 500+ students and young innovators. Turn a raw idea into a working prototype in days, guided by mentors and real SDG challenge briefs.

Camp Imagery
Overview

Innovation Camp is where Baguio's youth stop waiting for permission to build. Over an intensive three-day sprint, participants form teams, tackle a real challenge brief aligned to the city's priority research areas, and ship a working prototype.

No prior startup experience is required, just curiosity and a willingness to make something. The best teams are fast-tracked into the Startup Incubation Program.

How the camp runs
1

Day 1 · Discover & team up

Challenge briefs are revealed, teams form around problems, and mentors run a design-thinking kickoff.

2

Day 2 · Build the prototype

Teams prototype with mentor check-ins, technical clinics, and access to tools and materials.

3

Day 3 · Pitch & prizes

Teams pitch to a panel; winners take prizes and an invitation to incubation.

Sample challenge briefs
Food security

Reduce post-harvest loss for Cordillera highland vegetable farmers.

Environment

Help Baguio manage waste and protect its watersheds.

Tourism & creative

Showcase Baguio's creative economy beyond peak season.

Education

Improve access to learning for remote barangay students.
