Cut post-harvest loss for highland vegetable farmers

DA
Dept of Agriculture, CAR
Government · Challenge poster

About this challenge

Benguet province is one of the Philippines' primary vegetable-producing regions, yet a significant portion of its harvest is lost before reaching Metro Manila and other markets. Cold-chain gaps, poor packaging, and inefficient logistics mean that farmers lose income and consumers pay more for less.

We are looking for a solution — a product, platform, process, or service — that meaningfully reduces post-harvest loss for highland vegetable growers. Solutions should be feasible to deploy in the Cordillera, appropriate for smallholder farmers, and scalable.

What we're looking for

✓A cold-chain, packaging, or logistics innovation that reduces spoilage during transport from farm to market.
✓A digital tool or platform that connects farmers directly to buyers, reducing middlemen and spoilage from delays.
✓Solutions must be cost-appropriate for smallholder farmers and deployable within the Cordillera region.
✓A working prototype or pilot evidence is a strong advantage in the evaluation.

Evaluation criteria

Feasibility
Can this be built and deployed in the Cordillera with existing resources?
Impact
How many farmers and households does it benefit? By how much?
Scalability
Can the solution grow beyond a single barangay or municipality?
Team
Does the team have the right mix of skills and access to execute?

Submit your solution before Aug 15. The DA-CAR team will review all entries.

About Incubator Baguio

Baguio's Innovation Alliance

Incubator Baguio brings together government, academia, industry, entrepreneurs, researchers, and community partners to strengthen innovation, entrepreneurship, and startup development in the city.

Our Role

Building Connections Across the Innovation Ecosystem

Innovation does not happen in isolation. Entrepreneurs need mentors, researchers need industry partners, startups need support networks, and institutions need platforms for collaboration.

Incubator Baguio serves as a shared platform where government agencies, higher education institutions, private organizations, startups, and innovators can work together to develop ideas, strengthen capabilities, and create new opportunities.

Our Foundation

Institutionalized Through City Ordinance

Incubator Baguio was established through Ordinance No. 063, Series of 2023, which created the Baguio City Research and Innovation Alliance under the City Planning, Development and Sustainability Office (CPDSO).

The ordinance provides the foundation for long-term collaboration among government, academia, industry, and the community to strengthen innovation, entrepreneurship, research, and startup development in Baguio.

Innovation Camp

A high-energy annual immersion for 500+ students and young innovators. Turn a raw idea into a working prototype in days, guided by mentors and real SDG challenge briefs.

Camp Imagery
Overview

Innovation Camp is where Baguio's youth stop waiting for permission to build. Over an intensive three-day sprint, participants form teams, tackle a real challenge brief aligned to the city's priority research areas, and ship a working prototype.

No prior startup experience is required, just curiosity and a willingness to make something. The best teams are fast-tracked into the Startup Incubation Program.

How the camp runs
1

Day 1 · Discover & team up

Challenge briefs are revealed, teams form around problems, and mentors run a design-thinking kickoff.

2

Day 2 · Build the prototype

Teams prototype with mentor check-ins, technical clinics, and access to tools and materials.

3

Day 3 · Pitch & prizes

Teams pitch to a panel; winners take prizes and an invitation to incubation.

Sample challenge briefs
Food security

Reduce post-harvest loss for Cordillera highland vegetable farmers.

Environment

Help Baguio manage waste and protect its watersheds.

Tourism & creative

Showcase Baguio's creative economy beyond peak season.

Education

Improve access to learning for remote barangay students.
